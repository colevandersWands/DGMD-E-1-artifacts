# Embodying TCER: Embody as Translational Computing Education Research

**A comprehensive analysis of how Embody exemplifies and extends the Translational Computing Education Research (TCER) model**

---

## Executive Summary

This directory documents a deep analysis of how the Embody project operates within the **[Translational Computing Education Research (TCER)](http://youtube.com/watch?v=L9HTo17Y5gw)** framework proposed by Cole, Malaise, and Signer (2023). Through systematic examination, we reveal that Embody:

1. **Spans multiple TCER phases simultaneously** (Phases 1.B, 3.A, 3.B, 4.A) rather than fitting into a single activity
2. **Operates in trading zones** where research and practice coordinate, exemplifying the translational spectrum
3. **Demonstrates tool-theory co-evolution** where building infrastructure IS research contribution, not just research application
4. **Both IS and ENABLES Translational Sprints** - developed through agile CER methodology while accelerating future research

This analysis serves two purposes:

- **Theoretical**: Understanding Embody's research contributions through the TCER lens
- **Practical**: Guiding how to reframe existing documentation (especially `theory-to-requirements/`) to reflect its translational research nature

## Key Insights Summary

### 1. Multi-Phase Translational Work

Embody simultaneously operates in Phases 1.B, 3.A, 3.B*, 4.A*. This is expected for translational researchâ€”the bidirectional arrows exist for projects like this.

### 2. Trading Zones Enable Coordination

Phase 4.A\* enables diverse stakeholders to coordinate locally without global consensus. Embody remains theory-neutral while serving multiple pedagogies.

### 3. Tool-Theory Co-evolution

Building measurement infrastructure creates theoretical insights, validates theories, and reveals research priorities. Like CERN for physics.

### 4. Translational Sprints Operationalize TCER

TCER tells you WHAT (framework). Translational Sprints tell you HOW (methodology). Embody both IS a sprint and ENABLES future sprints.

### 5. Infrastructure Accelerates Research

Shared measurement infrastructure eliminates 6-12 month bottleneck per project, enables multi-institution coordination, builds cumulative knowledge.
